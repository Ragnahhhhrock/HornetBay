Angle deck, a working meatball, and a burner tell d0ee2a7

The carrier finally looks like a carrier. The landing strip now runs onto a proper port-side sponson — every full-deck carrier angles its landing deck to the left — with edge stripes and lights to match, and the deck footprint for traps extends over it. Bolted to the port edge abeam the wires is a functional IFLOLS: an amber ball rides five cells against the green datum row to show where you sit on the 3.5° glideslope, and red wave-off lamps flash if you come aboard dirty (gear or hook up) or badly off slope. Two smaller tells: at 100% thrust with the burner lit, the cockpit thrust readout gets the original's bright yellow underline, and the aircraft position lights, strobes and beacons are shrunk to realistic size.
